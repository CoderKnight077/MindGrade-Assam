Q. 1. Answer these questions to check your understanding.


(a) When do Kashmiri vendors travel to-cities to sell their goods?

Ans: At the end of rainy season and the beginning of winter season the kashmiri vendors travel to- cities to sell their goods.

(b) Why is it a hard life for the Kashmiri Vendors?

Ans :- Ans : It is hard life for the kashmiri vendors because they have to travel around the city on foot,
carrying a bundle of things to sell their products. Moreover they stay in cheap hotels and they eat something
in the morning and spend the rest of the day roaming in the city remaining hungry and thirsty.

(c) Why was the author surprised when his brother in law called the vendors?



Ans : The author was surprised when his brother-in-law called the vendors because the author knew that he needed nothing from them, they all knew that brother-in-law wouldn’t buy anything from them.

(d) “I felt sad and guilty at being a party to such behaviour.”- What does the word ‘party’ mean here?


Ans: The party here means to take sides. The author refers to the behaviour of his brother-in-law. Who called the kashmiri vendors but everyone knew that he didn’t need anything from them.



(e) Who was kind? What was his deed of kindness?


Ans: The brother-in-law of the author was kind. His deed of kindness was to offer food to the two Kashmiri vendors.
